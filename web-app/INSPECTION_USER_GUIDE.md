# Inspection Workflow - User Guide

## For Mechanics

### Starting an Inspection

1. **Access the Inspection Form**
   - Navigate to the booking details
   - Click "Start Inspection"
   - The inspection form loads with all component categories

2. **Understanding the Status Options**

   | Status | Icon | Meaning | Action |
   |--------|------|---------|--------|
   | **Good** | ✓ | Component working properly | No maintenance needed |
   | **Fair** | ⚠️ | Some wear but functional | Schedule maintenance soon |
   | **Poor** | ✗ | Damaged or failing | Immediate action needed |
   | **Not Checked** | ○ | Not inspected | Will not affect score |

3. **Inspecting Each Component**

   For each category (Engine, Brakes, Tyres, etc.):

   1. **Select Status**: Choose the component condition
   2. **Add Comments**: Write detailed observations
   3. **Set Severity** (if Poor): Mark as Low/Medium/High/Critical
   4. **Recommended Action**: What should be done
   5. **Upload Images**: Add up to multiple images per category

### Taking & Uploading Images

**Best Practices:**
- Take clear, well-lit photos
- Include multiple angles (front, side, close-up)
- Zoom in on problem areas
- Include reference object for scale

**Image Requirements:**
- Formats: JPG, PNG, WebP
- Size: Automatically compressed to max 1MB
- Quantity: No limit per category

**Upload Process:**
1. Click "Upload Images" button
2. Select one or multiple images
3. Images are previewed as thumbnails
4. Remove incorrect images using the X button
5. Proceed with inspection

### Severity Levels for Poor Status

- **Low**: Minor issue, can wait 2-4 weeks
- **Medium**: Moderate issue, schedule within 1-2 weeks
- **High**: Significant issue, schedule within 3-7 days
- **Critical**: Dangerous issue, immediate action needed

### Completing the Inspection

1. **Review Progress**: Check the progress bar
2. **Add Overall Notes**: General observations about the vehicle
3. **Submit**: Click "Complete Inspection"
4. **System Generates**:
   - Health Score (0-100)
   - Safety Score
   - Performance Score
   - Maintenance Recommendations
   - Repair Cost Estimate

### Understanding the Report

After submission, review the auto-generated report showing:

**Scores:**
- Overall Health: Complete vehicle health
- Safety Score: Critical component condition
- Performance Score: Engine/drivetrain health

**Condition Levels:**
- Excellent (85-100): No concerns
- Good (70-84): Monitor during next service
- Fair (50-69): Plan maintenance
- Poor (30-49): Schedule urgent service
- Critical (0-29): Safety risk

**Recommendations:**
- Organized by priority (Critical → Low)
- Include estimated costs
- List required parts
- Show urgency timeline

### Sharing Reports with Customers

1. **Download PDF**: Click "Download PDF" for professional report
2. **Print**: Click "Print" for physical copy
3. **Email**: Send digitally to customer
4. **In-App**: Customer can view in app notification

## For Customers

### Viewing Your Vehicle's Inspection

#### Latest Inspection Results

Access the inspection report through:
- Dashboard → Your Vehicle
- Click "View Latest Inspection"
- Or navigate directly: Inspections → View Report

#### Understanding Your Health Score

```
Score Range  | Condition        | What It Means
85-100       | Excellent ✓✓    | Vehicle is in great condition
70-84        | Good ✓          | Minor maintenance may be needed
50-69        | Fair ⚠️         | Schedule service soon
30-49        | Poor ✗          | Important issues to address
0-29         | Critical ✗✗     | Safety concerns - urgent action
```

#### Health Score Explained

The Health Score combines the condition of all inspected components:
- **Engine**: How well it runs
- **Brakes**: Stopping safety
- **Tyres**: Traction and wear
- **Battery**: Electrical reliability
- **Oil**: Lubrication quality
- **Lights**: Visibility and signals

### Acting on Recommendations

#### Priority Levels

| Priority | Icon | Timeline | Action |
|----------|------|----------|--------|
| **Critical** | 🔴 | Within 1 day | Book immediately |
| **High** | 🟠 | Within 7 days | Schedule this week |
| **Medium** | 🟡 | Within 2 weeks | Schedule soon |
| **Low** | 🟢 | Within 30 days | Monitor/schedule |

#### Each Recommendation Includes

- **What needs fixing**: Clear description
- **Why it matters**: Impact on safety/performance
- **Estimated cost**: Budget planning
- **Required parts**: What will be replaced
- **Urgency**: How soon to schedule

### Inspection History & Trends

#### Accessing History

1. Go to Your Vehicles
2. Select a vehicle
3. Click "View Inspection History"

#### Understanding Trends

**Improving Score** ⬆️ Green Arrow
- Your vehicle is getting better
- Good maintenance practices
- Keep current service schedule

**Stable Score** ➡️ No Arrow
- Vehicle condition unchanged
- Continue regular maintenance
- Monitor specific areas

**Declining Score** ⬇️ Red Arrow
- Vehicle needs attention
- Schedule service soon
- Follow mechanic recommendations

#### Timeline View

- See past 20 inspections
- Compare with previous inspections
- Track component changes
- Identify patterns

### What to Do After Inspection

#### If Score is Good/Excellent

✓ Continue regular maintenance schedule
✓ Monitor seasonal changes
✓ Keep inspection records

#### If Score is Fair

⚠️ Review recommendations
⚠️ Schedule maintenance within 2 weeks
⚠️ Budget for suggested repairs

#### If Score is Poor/Critical

✗ Contact service center immediately
✗ Follow safety recommendations
✗ Schedule urgent repairs
✗ Don't ignore critical warnings

## Common Inspection Findings

### Engine

**Good Condition:**
- Smooth operation
- No unusual noises
- Oil level normal
- No leaks

**Action Needed:**
- Knocking sounds
- Overheating
- Oil leaks
- Rough idling

### Brakes

**Good Condition:**
- Responsive braking
- No noise/grinding
- Sufficient pad thickness
- Fluid level normal

**Action Needed:**
- Soft/spongy pedal
- Grinding sounds
- Visible pad wear
- Fluid leak

### Tyres

**Good Condition:**
- Proper pressure
- Even tread wear
- No damage
- Sufficient tread depth

**Action Needed:**
- Low pressure
- Uneven wear
- Visible damage
- Low tread depth

### Battery

**Good Condition:**
- Clean terminals
- Proper voltage
- Secure connection
- No corrosion

**Action Needed:**
- Corrosion
- Loose terminals
- Weak voltage
- Difficulty starting

### Oil Condition

**Good Condition:**
- Golden color
- Proper level
- On service schedule

**Action Needed:**
- Dark/black color
- Low level
- Over service interval
- Burnt smell

### Lights

**Good Condition:**
- All lights working
- Proper brightness
- No cracks/damage

**Action Needed:**
- Burnt out bulbs
- Dim lights
- Cracked lenses
- Loose connections

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How often should I get an inspection?

**A:** 
- Routine: Every 6 months or 10,000 km
- If score is Fair/Poor: Follow recommendations (1-4 weeks)
- After major repairs: Confirm fixes worked

### Q: What if I don't agree with the inspection results?

**A:** 
1. Request a second inspection
2. Discuss specific findings with mechanic
3. Get detailed photos/explanations
4. Escalate to management if needed

### Q: Are inspection recommendations mandatory?

**A:** 
No, but:
- Follow safety recommendations immediately
- Medium/High priority repairs should be scheduled soon
- Ignoring recommendations may void warranty

### Q: How are repair costs estimated?

**A:** 
Estimates are based on:
- Component type
- Severity of condition
- Standard parts pricing
- Labor rates

Actual costs may vary. Get detailed quote before repair.

### Q: Can I download/print my inspection report?

**A:** 
Yes! The report includes:
- All inspection details
- Health scores
- Recommendations
- Estimated costs
- Professional formatting

Use for insurance/warranty claims.

### Q: What does "Not Checked" mean for a component?

**A:** 
- Component wasn't inspected in this visit
- Doesn't affect health score calculation
- May be checked in future inspections
- Request inspection of specific areas if needed

### Q: How is the health score calculated?

**A:** 
Health Score weights components by importance:
- **Critical (33%)**: Brakes, Lights, Battery
- **Important (33%)**: Engine, Tyres
- **Standard (34%)**: Oil, General

All weighted by actual status → Average = Health Score
